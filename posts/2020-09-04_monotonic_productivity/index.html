Monotonic vs. non-monotonic productivity increases

2020-09-04

I sometimes feel like the productivity boosts promised by Emacs/Vim evangelists is something of a red herring: I find myself spending a lot of time tweaking my configuration files, or researching why a feature or function does not work as I expect it to. This is the antithesis of productivity for me: Instead of writing, I spend my time reading documentation.

I like tools and hacks that make my life easier right away, even if the productivity boosts are very minor. I jokingly refer to such hacks as providing monotonic productivity increases: My day-to-day productivity will never decrease as a result of using them. For instance, I use a hotkey (alt + space ) to move to an iTerm2 terminal window in macOS. From there, I can type bib $ARG and hit enter, which automatically opens a browser window, heads my institution’s library website, and searches all available literature databases for $ARG. This extremely simple hack allows me to very quickly check whether a book or article that I’m interested in is available via my university. Surprisingly convenient. I’d consider this to be a monotonic productivity increase (at least, I suppose, until the script someday breaks down for whatever idiotic reason).
